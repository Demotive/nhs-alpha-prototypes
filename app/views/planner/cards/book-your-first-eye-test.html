Diabetes can damage your blood vessels which can cause sight problems (diabetic retinopathy) and blindness. An eye check can detect damage before you notice changes to your sight. Treating damaged blood vessels early can stop them affecting your sight.

You should book your first eye test as soon as you are diagnosed with type 2 diabetes. After that, you should have another eye test every year.

At the test the optician will put drops in your eyes to enlarge your pupils, and will then take a photo of the back of your eye. The whole test should take around 30 minutes.
